President of India

        
        
The President of India is the head of state of the Republic of India. The President is the nominal head of the executive, the first citizen of the country, as well as the supreme commander of the Indian Armed Forces. Droupadi Murmu is the 15th and current President, having taken office from 25 July.

    


    

        
Prime Minister of India

        
        
The Prime Minister of India is the head of the government of the Republic of India. Executive authority is vested in the Prime Minister and his chosen council of ministers, despite the President of India being the nominal head of the executive. The Prime Minister has to be a member of one of the houses of the bicameral Parliament of India, alongside heading the respective house. The Prime Minister and his cabinet are at all times responsible to the Lok Sabha. Shri Narendra Modi is the current Prime Minister of India.

    


    

        
Council of Ministers

        
        
The Union Council of Ministers, also called the Union Cabinet, is the principal executive organ of the Government of India, which functions as the senior decision-making body of the executive branch. It is chaired by the Prime Minister and consists of the heads of each of the executive government ministries. Currently, the Council is headed by Prime Minister Narendra Modi and consists of 29 members, including the Prime Minister. The Council is subject to the Parliament of India.

Lok Sabha

        
        
The Lok Sabha, or House of the People, is the lower house of Parliament. Members of Lok Sabha are directly elected by the people of India.

    


    

        
Rajya Sabha

        
        
The Rajya Sabha, or Council of States, is the upper house of Parliament. Members of Rajya Sabha are elected by the elected members of State Legislative Assemblies.

    Judicial System of India
    
    
    
    The Indian judiciary is a single integrated system. The constitution divides the judiciary into two parts: the superior judiciary (consisting of the Supreme Court and the High Courts) and the subordinate judiciary (the lower courts, which are under the supervision of the High Courts).
